# Audit Memo

`tau2-bench` retail does not grade the behaviors that matter most for Tenacious, and the Week 10 evidence makes that gap concrete. The saved retail run referenced by `week_10_data/trace_log.jsonl` contains real simulation IDs such as `f66839d0-fd13-4b1b-9eac-ad11836811df`, `ec9f91d2-3bc2-46c9-842c-59349f204db1`, `92662dc5-0856-4048-a24e-a1692c9f65fb`, `16592a4c-6b65-40fe-9a0b-01e40a965172`, and `c8a367b4-dee9-4a4a-ad5c-8dccd6b06125`. Those traces evaluate authentication, order lookup, cancellation, return, and exchange behavior in a retail support domain. They do not inspect whether an email is grounded in a hiring signal, whether a staffing promise exceeds the bench, whether a competitor-gap claim is sourced, or whether a booking CTA appears before the prospect has actually signaled readiness.

The Week 10 Tenacious-specific probe bundle shows exactly what the retail benchmark misses. Throughout this memo, the Tenacious trace evidence refers to the Week 10 probe traces listed in `week_10_data/probe_library.md`, not the retail benchmark simulations in `trace_log.jsonl`.

First, the highest-cost failures are hard business-rule violations. In `P-009`, `P-010`, `P-011`, and `P-012`, the system promises staffing or timing that the bench does not support. Five specific Week 10 Tenacious trace IDs make that gap concrete: `probe-4087895185a9` and `probe-c1a89e56414b` (`P-009`, promising 10 Go engineers when the bench has 3), `probe-d5299b421fc8` (`P-010`, treating committed NestJS engineers as available), `probe-258f1573489a` (`P-011`, promising 2 senior ML engineers when the bench has 1), and `probe-21a138e1feac` (`P-012`, promising infra deployment inside a 7-day window despite a 14-day lead time). This cluster maps directly to `bench_overcommitment`, which the taxonomy records at **40/40 (100%)**. A public benchmark that only checks whether the system completed a generic task cannot tell the difference between a fluent draft and a commercially dangerous overcommitment.

Second, the benchmark misses Tenacious-specific segmentation logic. `P-001` and `P-004` show deterministic ICP routing failures: a layoff-plus-funding prospect is pitched as a growth buyer, and a funded company with zero open engineering roles still passes Segment 1 screening. The trace references `probe-8dc44eb36d33`, `probe-bde8ece3a8ff`, and `probe-19f0af95e3e2` show the failure at the evidence level, while the taxonomy records `icp_misclassification` at **20/37 (54%)**. Retail-support benchmarks do not encode this kind of sales posture logic, so they cannot reward correct handling or penalize incorrect routing.

Third, the benchmark misses confidence-sensitive grounding. `P-005`, `P-027`, and `P-028` show that the public-signal pipeline can either overstate confidence or inherit noisy evidence. The relevant trace refs include `probe-b3388b3c3582`, `probe-a9b6ee255602`, `probe-ce2699cfff77`, and `probe-1fedf4a91d6b`. Tenacious needs the agent to say "I may be over-reading this" when the signal is weak, and it needs upstream signal computations not to hallucinate readiness. Retail-support tasks do not test either behavior because they do not have a notion of confidence-calibrated outreach or prospect-quality scoring.

Fourth, the benchmark misses sourced and non-condescending competitor-gap behavior. `P-029`, `P-030`, and `P-031` show unsupported or poorly framed gap claims, with direct Week 10 trace refs including `probe-2a2daa64075d` and `probe-46a313bfdec2` for sample-benchmark reuse, plus the broader `gap_overclaiming` cluster in the taxonomy. This is a Tenacious-specific trust problem: the draft may be grammatical and goal-directed while still being commercially unusable because the peer evidence is unsourced or framed as "you are behind."

Finally, the benchmark under-represents conversation-stage policy. `P-022` and `P-023` target premature booking and missing re-engagement. These are not generic dialogue problems; they are sales-process problems tied to when a booking link is permitted and when a dormant thread should be reopened. Again, a public benchmark that checks generic completion misses whether the agent moved at the right stage.

The implication is straightforward. Tenacious needs a benchmark whose unit of value is not generic task completion but policy-faithful sales behavior: grounded signals, correct segment routing, bench-safe commitments, source-aware competitor framing, and stage-appropriate CTA decisions. That is the gap Tenacious-Bench v0.1 is designed to close.
